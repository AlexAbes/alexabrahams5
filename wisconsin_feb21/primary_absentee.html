Take-aways: The counties with the biggest numbers of returned ballots are Dane and Waukesha, indicating some Democratic enthusiasm for the primary election. However, Waukesha and Brown also have decent numbers, so turnout may not be entirely lopsided. Given that the data has not been updated since 2/9, we cannot draw certain conclusions. There is no strong partisan pattern amongst the counties with the highest rates of balllot return; the bluer counties of Green and La Crosse have similar return rates so far to Sheboygan and Kewaunee counties. On the voter protection side, both statewide and in heavily Democratic counties like Dane and Milwaukee, county clerks have mailed out a high percentage of the absentee ballots that have been requested. However, voters in Dane and Milwaukee appear to have been mailing their ballots back at different rates; Dane is in line with the statewide average of 18% with a return rate of 19%, but Milwaukee is so far at only 10%. Milwaukee does have a relatively high number of in person absentee votes, which may be making up the difference if people do not trust USPS to get their ballots back in time.
